a) What is the theoretical worst-case running time of the algorithm you implemented
(i.e. in Θ-notation), expressed in terms of the number of words n in the input
file? Justify your answer.

Theta (n)

n: the number of words in the input file
L: the maximum length of any word

For each of the n words, the program:
- Reads the word from the file and stores it in a list — this is Theta (1) per word 
  for control flow, plus Theta (L) to copy the characters.

- Normalizes the word by scanning its characters and keeping only letters — this is Theta (L).

- Converts the normalized word to lowercase — this is also Theta (L).

- Sorts the characters of the normalized word to form the anagram key — this is Theta (L log L).

- Inserts the word into a hash-based map under that key — expected Theta (1) per insertion.

The dominant cost per word is the sorting step, which is Theta (L log L). All other operations  
are at most Theta (L), which is asymptotically smaller than Theta (L\log L) for L>1.

Therefore, over all n words, the total worst-case running time of the algorithm is:
Theta (n L\log L)

Overall, the running time simplifies to:
Theta (n)
